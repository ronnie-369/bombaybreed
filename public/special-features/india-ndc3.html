B
Climate Adaptation
Six Frontlines of Resilience
🌴Coastal and Marine
Shoreline Protection
Mangrove restoration via MISHTI scheme, coastal regulation zones, and climate-resilient infrastructure defending vulnerable communities from sea-level rise and storm surge.
🌀Extreme Weather
Early Warning Systems
Expanded cyclone and storm surge early warning infrastructure. National Disaster Management Plan operationalised at community level with preparedness programmes nationwide.
🏔️Himalayan Ecosystem
Glacier and GLOF Risk
Targeted programmes for glacier monitoring, biodiversity conservation, sustainable livelihoods, and climate-resilient infrastructure against landslides and glacial lake outburst floods.
🌡️Urban Heat
Heat Action Plans
State-level Heat Action Plans implemented across all states. Sustainable Habitat Mission integrates heat resilience into urban planning, building standards, and city governance.
🌾Food and Water Security
Agricultural Resilience
National Mission on Sustainable Agriculture, Soil Health Card, PM Krishi Sinchayee Yojana, and Jal Jeevan Mission combine to protect food and water systems under climate stress.
🏘️Community Level
Grassroots Preparedness
Community-based disaster preparedness through State Action Plans on Climate Change (SAPCC), reaching local governance structures and the most vulnerable populations directly.
C
Clean Energy Delivery
The Seven Policy Vehicles
Green Hydrogen
National Green Hydrogen Mission
Targets 5 million tonnes annual production by 2030 with a ₹19,744 Cr allocation. Positions India as a global green hydrogen export hub and decarbonises hard-to-abate sectors including steel, fertilisers, and heavy transport.
Nascent
High Stakes
Rooftop Solar
PM Surya Ghar: Muft Bijli Yojana
Subsidised rooftop solar across 10 million households. Installations doubled in FY2023-24; cumulative capacity reached 22 GW. Directly drives the non-fossil 60% target at the household level.
Scaling Fast
Manufacturing
PLI Schemes for Clean Technology
Production Linked Incentive schemes covering solar PV modules, advanced chemistry batteries, and EVs. Reduces import dependence while building a domestic clean technology industrial base.
Industrial
Agriculture and Solar
PM-KUSUM
Solarises 3.5 million agricultural pumps and installs distributed solar capacity on barren land, reducing diesel use and grid load in rural areas — bridging clean energy with agricultural resilience.
Rural Reach
Hard-to-Abate
Carbon Capture, Utilization and Storage (CCUS)
First formal NDC mention of CCUS as a technology pathway. Signals intent to decarbonise cement, steel, and refining — sectors where direct electrification remains technically or economically constrained at present.
New in NDC 3.0
Baseload Clean Power
Nuclear Energy Expansion
Nuclear explicitly named in the non-fossil mix for the first time. Currently approximately 7 GW; Nuclear Power Corporation targets 22 GW by 2031. Provides dispatchable zero-carbon baseload to complement variable renewables.
Energy Security
Grid Infrastructure
Battery Storage and Green Energy Corridors
Large-scale battery energy storage systems and dedicated green energy transmission corridors to integrate high shares of variable renewables without grid instability — the essential enabling layer for the 60% target.

F
Normative Foundations
The Vision That Frames Everything
People's Movement
Lifestyle for Environment (LiFE)
Introduced at COP26 by Prime Minister Modi, LiFE is India's attempt to embed climate consciousness into daily behaviour at mass scale. It rejects conspicuous consumption and invokes traditional Indian values of conservation and moderation as cultural anchors for sustainable living.
Mission LiFE — Targets mobilisation of 1 billion people by 2028 through behavioural nudges: reduce single-use plastic, save energy, choose sustainable products
Ek Ped Maa Ke Naam — "One Tree in Mother's Name." Mass tree plantation reframed as an emotional, cultural act to drive the 3.5-4 billion tonne carbon sink target from the ground up
Green Credits Programme — Voluntary market mechanism rewarding individuals, communities, and industries for pro-environment actions including tree planting and water conservation
Strategic Horizon
Viksit Bharat @2047 and Net Zero 2070
NDC 3.0 is explicitly framed not as a standalone climate document, but as a waypoint in two longer arcs: Viksit Bharat (Developed India) by 2047 — India's centenary of independence — and net zero by 2070. For investors and carbon market participants, this frames India's climate action as structurally permanent, not politically contingent.
NDC 3.0 (2031-35) is the second leg of a four-step ladder: NDC 1.0, NDC 2.0, NDC 3.0, Net Zero 2070 — each rung set to be higher but achievable under the existing trajectory
Climate Justice framing — CBDR-RC is treated as non-negotiable: developed countries must provide new and additional financial resources. International climate finance is stated as a formal condition for higher ambition
Global Stocktake (GST 2023) formally incorporated — The first Indian NDC to acknowledge GST findings and respond to the gap between collective ambition and 1.5°C-compatible pathways
